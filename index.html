Elijah Mears is a writer, a scholar, an amateur photographer, and a lifelong resident of North Carolina. A native of Cary, North Carolina, he was born January 3, 1995, and thus shares a birthday with both J.R.R. Tolkien and Marcus Tullius Cicero. He moved to Greensboro in 2013 for school and currently considers it to be his home. As a child, he dreamed of being a locomotive engineer, and though that is no longer his life aspiration, that dream still lives on in his love of public transit and train travel.

He is currently serving his second term as Director of Communications for the UNCG Democrats, as well as his first term as Chair of the Young Democrats of North Carolina LGBT+ Caucus. In addition to these roles with Democratic Party auxiliaries, he is also Vice President of the UNCG Classical Society.

Beyond politics, he is a 5th year Classical Languages & Literature major at UNC-Greensboro. His hobbies include writing, music composition, web design, and photography. He has had poetry published in The Coraddi, UNCG’s literary magazine, and has an article on Cicero forthcoming in a peer-reviewed undergraduate journal. He considers himself a strong but pragmatic progressive, and is deeply passionate about social justice, equitable wages, and public transportation access.
